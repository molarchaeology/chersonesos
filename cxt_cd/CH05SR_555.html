Description

  • CH05SR0555
  • Pit in 519 (E. Corner of Room 33), fill of pit 557, room 33
  • Grey-brown fine-silt-loam, loose ashy soil, approximately 60 cm in diameter, feature is generally circular. Visibly filled with metallurgical debris.

Interpretation

    • Filling of cut used as hearth feature. Bottom revealed two faces of hearth faces, one partial circle on a South -East, North-West orientation; the second better preserved had a circular back and was 20 cm wide by 30 cm long. Fearture was filled with a possible hearth bottom, which was sampled for Chris Salter. Appears to be single filling episode. Upon further articulation of the bedrock, a circular cut was discerned, probably associated with the features use as a hearth. Fill appears to be a single filling episode.
      • NK
    • Ceramic evidence suggests 13th c., though insufficient for certain conclusions (1 fr. local amphora of 13th -- class 52)
